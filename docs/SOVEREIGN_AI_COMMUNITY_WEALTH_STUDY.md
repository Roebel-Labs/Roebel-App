## Abstract

Rural Mecklenburg-Vorpommern has Germany's lowest incomes and a shrinking population; the wider economy is entering a period in which AI and automation may produce a growing share of value while the labour share of that value falls. Whoever **owns** the productive machinery of that economy captures its gains. This study proposes and tests a system of **structural techno-optimism**: an incentive design in which every citizen is measurably better off each time local technology advances, so that the community rationally *wants* more automation rather than resisting it.

The mechanism is an **upward spiral** of two coupled loops. An income loop — the community develops and exports genuine AI value (especially scarce *embodied*, physical-AI data and trained robot policies) and routes the revenue, via a citizen-governed treasury, into a progress-indexed dividend. And a cost loop — the community *owns* the automation and prices its output at cost, so local goods, energy, and services get cheaper. Progress therefore raises incomes and lowers prices at once, producing felt abundance and the will to advance further.

Both digital and embodied AI are treated as early points on a longer curve toward artificial general intelligence (AGI) and, later, superintelligence (ASI) — a curve along which the source of citizen income must shift from *contributing to* intelligence to *owning* it. We formalise the model — the spiral, the long arc, two engines (linear *circulation* and exponential *endowment*), three prosperity floors, and a constitutional 50/30/20 treasury split — and pre-register six hypotheses. The primary claim (H1) is that a community can develop and export real AI/robotics value at operating profit within 24 months; the defining claim (H6) is that tying citizen income to community-owned automation *inverts* the usual incentive to resist it. We present a bottom-up financial model with explicit assumptions, and are deliberately honest about what local operations can fund now (a meaningful supplement of order €40–200/month of real prosperity per citizen) versus full universal high income (a multi-decade compounding bet whose decisive variable is the endowment's growth rate as the AI economy scales). Röbel is run as an open, forkable, radically-documented experiment: what other communities adopt is not the data or the robots but the **credibly-neutral ruleset**. Funding is by open, international crowdfunding into a transparent community treasury.

**Keywords:** structural techno-optimism · sovereign AI · digital + embodied AI · AGI / ASI transition · ownership economy · regenerative finance · automation-ownership · credibly-neutral universal high income · community wealth fund · Circles v2 · MACI · community energy · abundance · x402

## 1 — Motivation: why here, why now

Two slow-moving facts collide in Röbel. The first is local: Mecklenburg-Vorpommern is the lowest-income German state, and small Müritz towns have lost people and prospects for a generation (roughly −0.6% population over five years locally). The second is global: as AI systems automate more cognitive and physical work, the value they create increasingly accrues to the owners of capital — models, compute, energy, data, robots — rather than to wage labour.

Superimposed, the default trajectory is grim for a town like Röbel: the economy grows more productive somewhere else, and the returns leave. This study investigates the **inversion**. If the community itself owns the AI, the compute, the robots, and the energy, every gain from automation flows *toward* citizens — as income, as cheaper essentials, as freed time. More automation then means a richer, freer town, not a hollowed-out one.

The bet is about **timing and ownership**. Stakes in the AI economy are cheapest before that economy has fully arrived, and the community that begins accumulating productive AI capital now holds a claim that compounds. Röbel already has the rare prerequisites: a live citizen-governed Gnosis multi-signature treasury (the *Gemeinschaftskasse* Safe), a working community currency with sybil-resistant personhood (CitizenNFT) and private voting (MACI), and an operating software team. The question is not *whether the rails exist* — they do — but **whether the incentive system can be made real enough, and the exported value genuine enough, to start the spiral described in §2.**

## 2 — The core mechanism: structural techno-optimism

Techno-optimism as a mood is hope. As a *system* it is an incentive structure in which progress pays each citizen directly. It runs on two coupled loops:

- **Income loop.** The community develops and exports genuine AI value (§5) → revenue and transaction fees → the treasury → a **progress-indexed dividend** whose size grows with deployed automation and export income. *Better technology raises income.*
- **Cost loop.** The community *owns* the automation and prices its output at cost (cooperative logic) → local produced goods, energy, and services get cheaper. *More automation lowers prices.*

Together: **progress → higher income *and* lower prices → felt abundance → more will and capital to advance technology.** That is the upward spiral.

**The incentive inversion (H6).** Everywhere else, automating your own task means losing your income, so workers rationally resist automation. Here, automating a task turns it into a *treasury-owned asset that pays everyone a dividend* — the displaced person keeps the dividend, the freed time, and the cheaper output. The private incentive to resist progress is removed. This is what turns a zero-sum fight into a spiral.

**The honesty constraint.** A spiral needs real fuel; a heat engine needs a temperature gradient. If citizens were paid from a closed loop of self-minted tokens, it would net to zero (the faucet problem). The spiral is real **only to the extent the community exports genuine value and captures real productivity gains.** Every claim in this paper is therefore anchored to externally-verifiable revenue and measured cost reduction, not token issuance.

### 2.1 The long arc: from earning intelligence to owning it

Both digital and embodied AI matter, and both are early points on a longer curve: narrow digital tools today, physical/embodied AI next, and — on the trajectory many expect — **artificial general intelligence (AGI)** able to perform most economic work, and later **artificial superintelligence (ASI)** producing the bulk of economic value with little human labour. The community's design must be robust across that entire arc, because the arc changes *where income comes from*:

- **Today — contribution.** Humans are essential inputs: they generate data, train and supervise models and robots, and do the physical tasks. Citizens earn mainly through *participation income* — Proof-of-Contribution to Intelligence (§6).
- **AGI — transition.** As AI performs more of the work, the marginal value of human labour falls. If income stayed tied to labour, citizens would lose. The 50/30/20 rule is built for exactly this: as contribution income declines, the *ownership dividend* — a share of the treasury's growing stock of owned AI, robots, compute, and energy — rises to replace it.
- **ASI — ownership.** In the limit where superintelligence produces most value, labour income approaches zero and the only durable claim on prosperity is *ownership*. A community that spent the early years accumulating productive AI capital, and that distributes its returns on a credibly-neutral, per-capita rule, converts the windfall into a genuine universal high income instead of watching it concentrate elsewhere.

So the system is a **bridge from a labour-income world to an ownership-income world**: early, you earn by helping build intelligence; late, you earn by owning it; the credibly-neutral dividend guarantees the transition is shared. This is why the endowment (Engine B), not this year's dividend, is the decisive long-run variable (H5).

**Sovereignty becomes safety, not only economics.** As the community's own AI grows more capable, governing it stops being merely an efficiency question. Community-owned, MACI-governed, values-aligned AI is a stance on *who controls increasingly powerful systems* — a small, accountable, human polity rather than a distant owner. The same constitution that splits the money also governs the agents; in the ASI limit, that governance is the most important thing the community owns.

## 3 — Hypotheses

We pre-register six hypotheses. **H1 is the primary outcome** (there is nothing to distribute without real earned value); **H6 is the defining mechanism** (the incentive inversion is what makes the system novel).

**H1 (primary — real earned value).** A community can develop and export genuine AI/robotics value — embodied-data and trained policies, plus bootstrap software services — at operating profit (revenue exceeding compute, staffing, and compliance cost) within 24 months.
*Falsified if* commercial revenue has not covered operating cost for three consecutive months by month 24.

**H2 (distribution).** A fixed on-chain 50/30/20 treasury rule delivers a measurable per-citizen dividend and a compounding endowment without ever drawing down principal.
*Falsified if* principal is spent to fund distributions, or the dividend never becomes measurable.

**H3 (two blades).** A cash dividend combined with community-owned essentials raises measured real prosperity per citizen more than an equivalent euro of cash transfer alone, because cheaper essentials also lower the denominator.
*Falsified if* the cost-of-living index does not fall for participating households, or the combined intervention shows no advantage over cash-only.

**H4 (sovereignty is sequenceable).** Renting German-hosted compute first (Hetzner) and owning local compute only where a genuine local need is proven yields equal or better margin than premature hardware ownership.
*Falsified if* owned compute is bought ahead of demand and sits underutilised, or renting cannot meet the data-sovereignty requirement.

**H5 (the long arc — monitored).** Over a multi-decade horizon spanning the digital → embodied → AGI → ASI transition, the endowment's growth *rate* — not its starting size — governs the path toward UHI-scale support, and the basis of citizen income shifts from contribution to ownership. Reinvestment plus AI-era returns compound faster than operations alone could distribute.
*Monitored, not settled in Phase A* — reported annually as realised compound growth vs. the modelled path, and as the ratio of ownership-dividend to contribution-income.

**H6 (the incentive inversion — the spiral).** Tying citizen income to community-owned automation makes participants measurably more pro-automation, and produces a *felt* rise in real prosperity (income up, essential costs down) as local technology advances.
*Falsified if* automating tasks does not raise the dividend or lower the cost-of-living index, or if participants resist community automation as workers do elsewhere.

## 4 — Background

The design builds on several literatures, none sufficient alone:

- **Sovereign wealth & permanent funds.** Alaska's Permanent Fund pays every resident an annual dividend (recently ~$1,700) from invested wealth; Norway's fund shows the discipline of spending only real returns. We adopt the model at municipal scale, substituting AI-era productive assets for oil. (Note: Alaska already proves *a* dividend — the novelty here is a *credibly-neutral, progress-indexed, community-owned* one; see §5.)
- **Unconditional transfers.** Germany's three-year basic-income pilot (€1,200/month, reported 2025) found no employment reduction and improved wellbeing; Stockton SEED and GiveDirectly Kenya show recipients shifting toward entrepreneurship and stability, not idleness.
- **Embodied-AI data.** Robot foundation models are bottlenecked on real-world manipulation and demonstration data; teleoperation and wearable-capture pipelines are an active, paid frontier — making community-collected embodied data a genuinely scarce, exportable asset rather than a commodity.
- **Community energy.** German *Bürgerenergiegenossenschaften* deliver electricity at ~4–9 ct/kWh vs ~39 ct retail — durable, legally-mature, revenue-generating, and the physical substrate of any local AI.
- **Cooperative & local-currency economics.** Mondragón, Preston community wealth building, and Sardinia's Sardex mutual-credit network show how value can be retained and recirculated locally rather than extracted.
- **Onchain primitives & credible neutrality.** Circles v2 (a demurrage group currency), MACI (private, collusion-resistant voting), Safe multi-signature custody, x402 (machine-native micropayments), and ERC-6551 (token-bound accounts) give programmable, auditable, sybil-resistant money, governance, and machine-owned assets — the instrumentation that makes techno-optimism a *ruleset* rather than a slogan.

A supply-side *abundance* framing (lower the real cost of essentials, not only raise incomes) and a deliberately inverted "network state" reading (build local prosperity and legitimacy first; recognition, if ever, last) complete the picture.

## 5 — The model: the spiral, two engines, three floors, one rule

**Two engines with different growth curves.** Engine A, *circulation*, is the community currency plus local operations; it grows roughly linearly and produces cash flow now. Engine B, the *endowment*, holds productive AI-era capital and compounds. The single most important structural fact: **Engine A's job is to buy Engine B** — present operations fund the acquisition of assets whose returns eventually dwarf those operations.

**Prosperity has two blades:**

```
Prosperity = ( income floor + citizen dividend ) ÷ cost of living
```

The spiral attacks both — the progress-indexed dividend lifts the numerator, community-owned automation lowers the denominator (H3, H6).

**Three floors beneath every citizen:**

| Floor | Name | What it is |
|---|---|---|
| 1 · Money | Participation income + dividend | Currency earned for real contribution to intelligence, plus a share of treasury profit |
| 2 · Abundance | Cheap community essentials | Community-owned energy, food, housing capacity, and automated services, priced at cost to members |
| 3 · Intelligence | An AI agent for every citizen | A sovereign model + compute quota as a public utility — the newest floor, treated as first-class |

**The constitution: a 50/30/20 split, written on-chain.** Every euro of net treasury inflow divides automatically:

```
50%  Dividend pool   ·   30%  Permanent endowment   ·   20%  Reinvest in local AI capital
```

Dividends grow linearly with operations; the endowment compounds with the AI economy; reinvestment feeds new revenue. Governance (MACI + timelock) may tune the ratios and smoothing parameters, but cannot alter a distribution already in flight and cannot spend endowment principal — **math sets the prices; votes only set the parameters.** This is what makes the dividend *credibly neutral*: per-capita, sybil-resistant, verifiable on-chain, ungameable by insiders. The first of its kind is the claim we test — not universal high income as such, which Alaska already demonstrates, but a *credibly-neutral, progress-indexed, community-owned* version of it.

**The progress-indexed dividend.** So that citizens *see* income rise with technology, the dividend pool is a published function of measured progress — deployed-automation value + AI/data export revenue + circulation velocity — rather than a fixed grant. Distribution uses an **epoch dividend auction**: each epoch the treasury funds a fixed pool `D` in EURe; verified citizens deposit currency (one claim lane per human, enforced by a Semaphore proof and a per-epoch nullifier, so the split is per-capita, not per-whale); at close the rate emerges by pro-rata division `rate = D ÷ total deposited`, deposits are burned, everyone claims. Self-balancing, un-drainable, oracle-free. It reframes "redemption" as what it is: **the citizen dividend, priced by demand and indexed to progress.**

**What is exported (the protocol, not the data).** Other communities adopt neither Röbel's datasets nor its robots but its **credibly-neutral ruleset** — the 50/30/20 constitution, the progress-indexed per-capita dividend, never-spend-principal. Röbel is genesis node #1 of a forkable protocol (POLIS). The growth engine is **demonstration + radical open documentation**, not sales.

## 6 — The engine that matters most: the community as an AI-development node

This is the load-bearing section: everything else distributes or compounds value this section must first *earn* from the outside world. The long-term thesis is **embodied intelligence**; software services are near-term **bootstrap fuel**, not the destination.

**6.1 Proof-of-Contribution to Intelligence (the core).** Citizens earn currency for developing AI, especially *physical* AI: AR-glasses task recordings, teleoperation demonstrations, robot supervision and edge-case correction, real-world task data, and labeling. The scarce input in robotics today is embodied/manipulation data; a community that systematically collects high-quality, consented real-world data holds a genuinely rare and sellable asset — and contributes real progress to the field. Contributions are attested (EAS), quality-scored against downstream model improvement, and rewarded in proportion to the value they add.

**6.2 Community-owned data & policies, exported via x402.** The datasets and trained robot policies are owned by a citizen-governed data trust and licensed/exported to labs and agents through machine-native micropayments (x402). A **transaction fee** on internal circulation and on exports funds the treasury. Data is sold consented and aggregated; provenance and consent are enforced by MACI/attestation.

**6.3 Build robots, automate local tasks.** The community builds and operates robots and automations (delivery, admin, agriculture, care support, energy operations). Each robot is a treasury-owned asset with a token-bound account (ERC-6551) streaming its net earnings to the dividend pool — so citizens can watch, on-chain, the machine source of their income. This is automation-ownership made literal: **the AI generates the value; the citizens hold the claim.**

**6.4 Bootstrap fuel — software services.** Near-term cash comes from legible software AI: a *Fördermittel* (grant-writing) agent that runs on Röbel's own behalf and as a service to other municipalities/Vereine; sovereign, GDPR-clean AI for regional SMEs (Handwerk, Gastro, Tourismus); and the white-label civic OS. These fund the embodied-AI flywheel until it carries itself — they are the means, not the mission.

**6.5 Go-to-market — the Trojan horse.** *Give away the thing; sell the scarce complement.* The free tier (app, AI assistant, robot-training tools, mini-apps) finds product-market fit **and** builds the moat — usage and the embodied-data corpus. The paid tier monetises what only that corpus makes possible: aggregated data, trained policies, AI-as-utility, automation-as-a-service, and the forkable node itself. Nobody pays upfront for something unproven, so free is not charity — it is how the sellable asset is built. Physical assets (community energy, robot fleet) are capitalised only after software profit and proven demand justify them: **demand-first, capital-last.**

| Line | Unit | Price / unit | Margin | Time-to-euro | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Bootstrap lines earn first and fund the core; the core (embodied data, agents, robots) is the long-term engine and the genuine contribution to the frontier.

All figures are **modelled scenarios with explicit assumptions, not forecasts.** Base assumes competent execution and the adoption ramp below; Conservative ≈ 0.5× and Optimistic ≈ 2× Base commercial revenue. Adoption is deliberately modest against today's reality (a handful of active citizens at the study's start). In this reframe, software lines dominate early and the embodied-data/robotics engine becomes the growth driver by Year 3–5.

Year-1 commercial revenue (€11.5k/mo) already exceeds operating cost (€7.5k/mo) — the H1 profitability test. Team scales 1.5 → 5 → 15 FTE. The embodied-data/robotics line is small early by design (the corpus and fleet are being built under the free tier) and is the scaling engine later.

|---|--:|--:|--:|

Per-citizen dividend stays ~€40–48 because the citizen base grows alongside revenue — by design: the dividend is a *share*, and growth is reinvested and endowed rather than paid out. Principal is never spent.

### 7.1 The cost-of-living blade changes the real number

A cash dividend of ~€45/month understates the intervention, because Floor 2 lowers the denominator. Community electricity at ~8 ct/kWh vs ~39 ct retail saves a typical household roughly **€77/month on power alone**; heat reuse, automated services, and food coupons plausibly bring total essential-cost reduction to **€120–150/month** by Year 3–5.

| Real prosperity gain per participating citizen (Yr 3–5) | € / month-equiv |
|---|--:|
| Cash dividend | ~€45 |
| Essential-cost reduction (energy, automated services, food) | ~€130 |
| **Total real prosperity gain** | **~€175** |

### 7.2 The honest gap to universal high income

A genuine UHI — say €1,500/month for 3,000 eligible citizens — is €54M/year, requiring roughly **€1.35 billion** in productive assets at a 4% real draw. The Year-5 endowment (~€1.7M) throws off ~€68k/year — real, but three orders of magnitude short. **Local operations do not reach UHI, and this study does not claim they will.** What they do is deliver a meaningful supplement now (~€175/month-equivalent) and — per H5 — fund the endowment whose compounding is the only credible long-arc path to the larger number. Whether that path closes is a decade-scale question contingent on AI-era returns; we monitor it, we do not promise it.

> **Honesty note.** Broad AI-equity exposure buys market *beta* — you ride the tide, you don't own the boat. The genuine power-law upside comes from equity in what Röbel itself incubates and from treasury-owned agents and robots, not from an index position. The model treats index exposure as ballast and local, owned intelligence as the source of asymmetric return.

## 8 — Methodology & funding: an open, longitudinal, internet-funded experiment

**Design.** A single-site, longitudinal, pre-registered field study with staged rollout. Unit of analysis: the participating verified citizen (and, for some measures, the household and the treasury). Because the town is the treatment, we use pre/post comparison against the town's own baseline plus a synthetic-control comparison from demographically comparable MV municipalities without the intervention.

**Phases (Phase A, 2026–2031):**

- **Phase 0 — Foundation (legally clean, months 0–6):** charter the Safe as the Röbel Permanent Fund with the on-chain 50/30/20 split; open **funding rails** — a public funding page accepting multichain crypto, bank/SEPA (a Monerium business IBAN so transfers arrive as EURe in the Safe), and Stripe, all as donations; ship the Fördermittel agent and the free citizen tier (app, AI assistant, first robot-training capture); stand up Hetzner-hosted sovereign inference for data-sensitive workloads; publish Prosperity Ledger v1 and the open documentation site. Donation-and-grant funded; avoids the regulated euro-out surface.
- **Phase 1 — First real value & abundance (year 1–2):** onboard paying software customers; launch Proof-of-Contribution-to-Intelligence data capture and first x402 exports; launch the community energy cooperative; begin the epoch dividend, paid first as **Abundance Coupons** (claims on below-market essentials) to stay clear of e-money law until cleared.
- **Phase 2 — Compounding (year 2–4):** first owned robots (demand-first); scale embodied-data exports; white-label to other towns; first spin-out equity into the endowment; euro-denominated dividend once legal clearance exists.
- **Phase 3 — Federation (year 4+):** shared inter-town endowment; treasury-owned autonomous agents and robot fleets at scale; the ruleset forked by further genesis nodes.

**Funding model.** Growth and capitalisation come from **radical open documentation + international crowdfunding**: everything is published; the live Prosperity Ledger is the proof; people who believe in the thesis fund the first credible demonstration. Donations are clean (a *gemeinnütziger* e.V. can issue *Spendenquittungen*); investment-for-return is regulated (VermAnlG/BaFin) and is deferred to a later, properly-structured vehicle (gGmbH + Schwarmfinanzierung exemption) — the funding page never blurs that line.

**Data, consent & labour ethics.** The primary instrument is on-chain and public, so core economic variables are audited by construction. Contribution-to-intelligence data (AR recordings, teleoperation) raises privacy, consent, and worker-classification questions (co-op member vs. employee); participation is opt-in, consented, MACI-private, framed as cooperative membership, and legally reviewed before any capture in private or commercial premises.

## 9 — Instrumentation: the Prosperity Ledger

What isn't measured can't be claimed. The Ledger publishes, live and on-chain wherever possible:

- **Treasury balance & inflows** — split into commercial revenue vs grants vs crowdfunding.
- **Commercial margin** — the H1 test, reported monthly, with the embodied-data/robotics share broken out.
- **Dividend per citizen** and **endowment value & realised real yield** (hard flag if principal moves).
- **Deployed-automation value & tasks automated** — the input to the progress-indexed dividend.
- **Human : machine productivity ratio** — treasury revenue from agents/robots vs people; rising over time is the automation-ownership thesis made visible.
- **Cost-of-living index** for participating households (the H3/H6 test) and **currency velocity** (the local-output proxy).
- **Sovereignty ratio** — share of AI queries served by the community's own model (the H4 test).
- **Adoption, wellbeing, and pro-automation sentiment** — active verified citizens; opt-in survey outcomes (the H6 test).

Publishing these openly is also the go-to-market: growth is *demonstration, not persuasion* — make Röbel visibly, measurably better off and let other towns fork the ruleset.

## 10 — Risks & threats to validity

- **The spiral needs real fuel.** If income comes from a closed token loop rather than exported value, it nets to zero. *Mitigation:* anchor every distribution to externally-verifiable revenue and measured cost reduction; measure velocity and export income, not token supply.
- **The embodied-data market must actually pay.** The core thesis assumes labs/agents buy the data and policies. *Mitigation:* Trojan-horse sequencing — build the corpus under the free tier, validate paying demand before capitalising the fleet.
- **Legal is the master gate.** Euro-denominated dividends, investment-style crowdfunding, a charity holding securities/robots, the energy cooperative, contribution-to-intelligence data capture, and worker classification each touch German regulation (BaFin / MiCA / e-money, VermAnlG, Gemeinnützigkeit, EnWG, GDPR, labour law). *Mitigation:* sequence by legal cleanliness — donations, grants, energy cooperative, coupon-denominated dividends first; euro-out, investment vehicles, and commercial data capture later, under a *Steuerberater* and *Rechtsanwalt*.
- **Housing won't fall from AI alone.** Automation lowers the cost of produced goods, energy, and services; land is scarce and regulated. *Mitigation:* the honest housing lever is a **community land trust** plus supply-building (modular / robot-assisted construction, zoning), not "AI makes housing cheap."
- **The AGI-timing bet (H5).** If AI value concentrates in inaccessible frontier labs, the endowment earns only beta. *Mitigation:* build machinery that pays regardless of AI timing; treat AI-beta as a long-duration option.
- **Adoption / empty-graph.** The model assumes an adoption ramp today's numbers don't yet show. *Mitigation:* ship a small but real dividend fast; make Floor 2 savings immediately felt; free tier lowers the join cost to zero.
- **Governance capture & credible-neutrality overclaim.** A treasury holding real assets is a target, and "first credibly-neutral UHI" is a strong claim. *Mitigation:* MACI private voting, timelock, multi-signature Safe, immutable 50/30/20, never-spend-principal — and earn the neutrality claim through verifiable on-chain rules rather than asserting it.
- **External validity.** One town, one founder, favourable initial conditions. *Mitigation:* the forkable ruleset is itself the replication protocol — each new node is an independent test.

## 11 — Conclusion

This is a study designed to run for years, not a launch plan dressed as one. Its wager is narrow and testable: that a small town can build an incentive system in which technological progress — digital and physical — makes every citizen visibly better off, so that the community *wants* to advance technology, owns the machinery that results, and shares the gains on a credibly-neutral basis. The near-term result we expect is honest and modest: operating-profitable community AI within two years (H1), the incentive inversion made real (H6), and a dividend-plus-cost-reduction worth on the order of €175/month-equivalent per citizen by year five. The far-term result is a bet on compounding we monitor in the open rather than assert.

Sovereign digital AI, embodied AI, regenerative finance, and community-owned sustainability are not four projects here; they are one flywheel — citizens grow intelligence, intelligence earns, the treasury allocates on a fixed neutral rule, essentials get cheaper, the endowment compounds, and the whole thing is documented openly so its success or failure is legible to anyone who wants to fork it. The deeper wager is on the arc: as intelligence runs from today's tools toward AGI and ASI, the community quietly converts its citizens from workers-who-earn into owners-who-share — so that when machines produce most of the value, the people still hold the claim. If the spiral turns, Röbel is not asking for a universal high income — it is building, owning, and democratically governing the machinery that pays one. Röbel is Genesis Node #1. The experiment begins now.
